Design communication

Design communication involves clearly explaining design concepts and decisions to different audiences.

HEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • clearly explain problems and design ideas to others
  • clearly explain design decisions to others using appropriate tools and methods

Service designers:

Junior designers create empathy maps and journey maps to understand user needs, conduct stakeholder mapping exercises, and use service safaris to experience services first-hand. They use Lucid for ecosystem mapping to visualise service contexts.

Interaction designers:

Junior designers apply GOV.UK Design System patterns strategically, document how interface choices support service goals in the Design History service, and participate in design system working groups to ensure consistency across services.

SEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • clearly explain problems and design ideas to others
  • clearly explain design decisions to others using appropriate tools and methods

Service designers:

Midweight designers facilitate service mapping workshops using methods like user journey mapping and touchpoint mapping, create value stream maps to identify pain points, and use service blueprinting to understand service operations and user interactions.

Interaction designers:

Midweight designers develop new components for the GOV.UK Design System with robust accessibility testing, use analytics to identify user pain points, and create detailed documentation of design decisions to mitigate implementation risks.

G7

Practitioner

Capabilities:

  • clearly explain complex problems and ideas to teams or stakeholders outside of design
  • clearly document and communicate design decisions, related risks and any unresolved issues
  • build consensus around a design approach, for example, by asking difficult questions and challenging assumptions

Service designers:

Senior designers lead hypothesis-driven design using methods like experience prototyping and service roleplay, create service ecosystem maps to identify gaps between products and services, and use contextual inquiry to understand service environments. They apply systems thinking to spot opportunities for improvement.

Interaction designers:

Senior designers develop pattern libraries based on user research insights, help teams implement evidence-based design practices, and create reusable interaction components that address common user needs.

G6 Lead

Expert

Capabilities:

  • gain support for design decisions and design strategy from senior leaders
  • communicate design strategy across organisational boundaries
  • coach others in how to effectively communicate design
  • develop or introduce more effective methods of communicating design and working in the open

Service designers:

Lead designers use advanced service design methods like systems mapping and service ecology mapping to identify strategic opportunities, develop frameworks for cross-service integration using co-design workshops and participatory design methods, and establish methods for measuring the collective impact of interconnected services.

Interaction designers:

Lead designers work with user researchers to analyse complex datasets, collaborate with business analysts to create and test design hypotheses, and partner with product managers to validate interface decisions through user testing.

G6 Head of Profession

Expert

Capabilities:

  • gain support for design decisions and design strategy from senior leaders
  • communicate design strategy across organisational boundaries
  • coach others in how to effectively communicate design
  • develop or introduce more effective methods of communicating design and working in the open

The Head of Profession:

The Head of Profession sets the DfE's strategic design vision, leading cross-government initiatives, and embedding evidence-based design across all programmes. They ensure design is integrated into the wider strategic direction of the organisation and foster collaboration across departments.

Designing strategically

Strategy involves creating a plan to achieve a team or organisation's objectives.

HEO

Awareness

Capabilities:

  • explain the importance of aligning to the strategy of an organisation
  • describe how your design work aligns to the wider goals and vision of your team
  • explain the value of using patterns and components in design

Service designers:

Junior designers map their work to DfE's strategic objectives using service blueprints, collaborate with policy teams to understand broader organisational goals, and use the GOV.UK Service Standard to ensure alignment with government-wide best practices.

Interaction designers:

Junior designers apply GOV.UK Design System patterns strategically, document how interface choices support service goals in the Design History service, and participate in design system working groups to ensure consistency across services.

SEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • align your design work to both the goals and vision of your team and the strategic objectives of your organisation
  • identify potential risks and use design to mitigate them
  • identify opportunities and use design to take advantage of them
  • contribute to the development of design patterns and components

Service designers:

Midweight designers contribute new service patterns to the DfE design system with clear documentation, analyse service performance data to identify improvement opportunities, and create risk mitigation strategies through service mapping exercises.

Interaction designers:

Midweight designers develop new components for the GOV.UK Design System with robust accessibility testing, use analytics to identify user pain points, and create detailed documentation of design decisions to mitigate implementation risks.

G7

Practitioner

Capabilities:

  • help a team understand how user-centred design helps it meet its goals
  • help teams align their work to the goals and vision of their organisation
  • use risks, opportunities and constraints in technology, systems and policy to shape design
  • identify and create new design patterns and components

Service designers:

Senior designers lead hypothesis-driven design using methods like experience prototyping and service roleplay, create service ecosystem maps to identify gaps between products and services, and use contextual inquiry to understand service environments. They apply systems thinking to spot opportunities for improvement.

Interaction designers:

Senior designers develop pattern libraries based on user research insights, help teams implement evidence-based design practices, and create reusable interaction components that address common user needs.

G6 Lead

Practitioner

Capabilities:

  • help a team understand how user-centred design helps it meet its goals
  • help teams align their work to the goals and vision of their organisation
  • use risks, opportunities and constraints in technology, systems and policy to shape design
  • identify and create new design patterns and components

Service designers:

Lead designers use advanced service design methods like systems mapping and service ecology mapping to identify strategic opportunities, develop frameworks for cross-service integration using co-design workshops and participatory design methods, and establish methods for measuring the collective impact of interconnected services.

Interaction designers:

Lead designers work with user researchers to analyse complex datasets, collaborate with business analysts to create and test design hypotheses, and partner with product managers to validate interface decisions through user testing.

G6 Head of Profession

Expert

Capabilities:

  • work with others to ensure design becomes part of the development and implementation of your organisation's strategy
  • work with leaders to ensure design is included in policy, technology and delivery processes early enough to inform effective decision-making
  • enable use of patterns and components across the organisation

The Head of Profession:

The Head of Profession sets DfE's design strategy, ensuring that evidence-based practices are embedded across design teams and the department, and leads initiatives to share insights across government.

Designing together

Designing together involves working with others to create better products and services.

HEO

Awareness

Capabilities:

  • explain different techniques for bringing together perspectives from multiple people to inform a design
  • explain the importance of constructive feedback in the design process

Service designers:

Junior designers actively participate in team design sessions, collaborate with content designers, business analysts, and developers in paired working sessions, and document design decisions in the Design History service to ensure clear communication of service goals.

Interaction designers:

Junior designers pair with developers and business analysts to implement designs, participate in user research sessions to understand user needs, and contribute constructive feedback in design crits using GOV.UK Design System principles.

SEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • effectively plan and run a design session with your team, users or stakeholders
  • identify and engage the right people throughout the design process
  • give and receive constructive design feedback

Service designers:

Midweight designers plan and facilitate design sprints with product managers and business analysts, organise regular design reviews with multidisciplinary teams, and create collaborative working agreements to address complex service challenges.

Interaction designers:

Midweight designers run structured design sessions with product managers and business analysts to refine user flows, create and maintain collaborative design documentation, and mentor junior designers in accessibility best practices.

G7

Practitioner

Capabilities:

  • advise others how to effectively plan and run design sessions with a team, users or stakeholders
  • adapt a design session to ensure you achieve a useful outcome
  • effectively involve the right people throughout the design process
  • work across team or profession boundaries, for example with policy teams

Service designers:

Senior designers establish cross-team working groups with product managers and business analysts, facilitate design workshops using structured methodologies, and create frameworks for collaborative service development.

Interaction designers:

Senior designers lead collaborative design workshops with product managers and business analysts, create processes for consistent interface development, and establish methods for sharing design patterns across services.

G6 Lead

Expert

Capabilities:

  • effectively plan and run design sessions that include senior leaders or stakeholders
  • help teams connect and work across organisational boundaries
  • coach other designers in how to involve stakeholders of any level in the design process
  • develop or introduce more effective ways to design together

Service designers:

Lead designers run strategic design sessions with senior stakeholders using established facilitation techniques, create collaboration frameworks for major transformation programmes, and establish cross-government communities of practice.

Interaction designers:

Lead designers establish design leadership forums with clear objectives, lead multi-disciplinary design projects using agile methodologies, and create innovation labs for testing new interaction patterns.

G6 Head of Profession

Expert

Capabilities:

  • effectively plan and run design sessions that include senior leaders or stakeholders
  • help teams connect and work across organisational boundaries
  • coach other designers in how to involve stakeholders of any level in the design process
  • develop or introduce more effective ways to design together

The Head of Profession:

The Head of Profession runs design leadership away days, creates department-wide collaboration strategies, and sets the strategic direction for design across DfE.

Designing for everyone

Designing for everyone involves creating products and services that are accessible and inclusive.

HEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • work with others to design and deliver inclusive, accessible and environmentally sustainable content or services that meet the needs of all users
  • ensure a design meets appropriate standards, for example accessibility regulations, with support

Service designers:

Junior designers follow WCAG guidelines in service design, collaborate with accessibility specialists to ensure inclusive service delivery, and document accessibility considerations in service blueprints.

Interaction designers:

Junior designers conduct accessibility testing using screen readers and other assistive technologies, ensure designs meet WCAG standards, and document accessibility features in the Design History service.

SEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • work with others to design and deliver inclusive, accessible and environmentally sustainable content or services that meet the needs of all users
  • ensure a design meets appropriate standards, for example accessibility regulations, with support

Service designers:

Midweight designers embed accessibility checkpoints in the design process, conduct inclusive design reviews with diverse user groups, and maintain documentation of accessibility requirements for service improvements.

Interaction designers:

Midweight designers run accessibility testing sessions with assistive technology users, create accessible prototypes using the GOV.UK Design System, and document accessibility considerations in design patterns.

G7

Practitioner

Capabilities:

  • advise teams on how to design inclusive, accessible and environmentally sustainable content or services
  • design and deliver ethical content or services that consider the personal and social context of users
  • ensure a design meets appropriate standards, for example accessibility regulations

Service designers:

Senior designers create inclusive design principles for teams, establish accessibility testing frameworks for services, and mentor others in implementing accessible service design practices.

Interaction designers:

Senior designers develop accessible interaction patterns, lead accessibility testing workshops, and create guidelines for ensuring interface accessibility across services.

G6 Lead

Expert

Capabilities:

  • measurably improve the practice of inclusive, accessible and environmentally sustainable design in multiple teams or across your organisation
  • help others understand and mitigate the influence of privilege and power on the design and delivery of content and services

Service designers:

Lead designers work with the head of profession to develop inclusive design strategies and standards, advocate for inclusive design at senior levels, and lead inclusive design initiatives across teams and government departments.

Interaction designers:

Lead designers lead initiatives to improve inclusive design practices, establish accessibility frameworks, and ensure that interaction design meets accessibility regulations across services.

G6 Head of Profession

Expert

Capabilities:

  • measurably improve the practice of inclusive, accessible and environmentally sustainable design in multiple teams or across your organisation
  • help others understand and mitigate the influence of privilege and power on the design and delivery of content and services

The Head of Profession:

The Head of Profession leads the creation and implementation of DfE's inclusive design strategy, sets department-wide accessibility standards, and advocates for inclusive design at the executive level.

Evidence-based design

Evidence-based design involves using data and research to inform design decisions.

HEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • analyse, synthesise and clearly explain evidence relevant to users or a service, for example, web analytics data
  • work with researchers or analysts to use evidence to inform, develop and test a design idea

Service designers:

Junior designers use data insights and feedback from user research to inform and develop their design decisions, ensuring user needs are central to the service strategy.

Interaction designers:

Junior designers use analytics and user feedback to refine interface designs, ensuring that interaction patterns meet user needs and improve the service's overall effectiveness.

SEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • analyse, synthesise and clearly explain evidence relevant to users or a service, for example, web analytics data
  • work with researchers or analysts to use evidence to inform, develop and test a design idea

Service designers:

Service designers contribute to research planning with user researchers, observe research sessions, and work with user researchers to make sense of research findings to inform service improvements.

Interaction designers:

Interaction designers ensure that research insights are embedded into design decision-making, lead retrospectives, and mentor other designers in using evidence to drive design improvements.

G7

Practitioner

Capabilities:

  • analyse, synthesise and clearly explain complex evidence relevant to users or a service, for example, large data sets
  • help your team use design hypotheses effectively
  • use complex research and data to develop and test design ideas

Service designers:

Service designers collaborate with user researchers to maintain research repositories, work with business analysts to establish metrics for measuring service performance, and partner with product managers to implement processes for sharing insights across government.

Interaction designers:

Interaction designers work with user researchers to analyse complex datasets, collaborate with business analysts to create and test design hypotheses, and partner with product managers to validate interface decisions through user testing.

G6 Lead

Expert

Capabilities:

  • embed a practice of hypothesis-driven design in a team
  • guide the organisation in how to use evidence to improve services
  • analyse, synthesise and use evidence to improve the way the organisation works
  • ensure user insights are shared with and used by the wider organisation and across government

Service designers:

Service designers lead research panels, create measurement frameworks, and set up processes to share research findings across teams and the wider organisation.

Interaction designers:

Interaction designers ensure that research insights are embedded into design decision-making, lead retrospectives, and mentor other designers in using evidence to drive design improvements.

G6 Head of Profession

Expert

Capabilities:

  • embed a practice of hypothesis-driven design in a team
  • guide the organisation in how to use evidence to improve services
  • analyse, synthesise and use evidence to improve the way the organisation works
  • ensure user insights are shared with and used by the wider organisation and across government

The Head of Profession:

The Head of Profession sets DfE's design strategy, ensuring that evidence-based practices are embedded across design teams and the department, and leads initiatives to share insights across government.

Iterative design

Iterative design involves creating and improving products and services through a process of testing and refinement.

HEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • apply iterative design principles and agile methodologies to your work
  • iterate and improve a design based on successive rounds of research
  • prototype your ideas at different fidelities to explore and test designs
  • use a range of design tools and techniques
  • use and iterate design patterns and components

Service designers:

Service designers iterate on service concepts and patterns, work with interaction designers to inform prototype requirements, and refine solutions based on feedback from user research.

Interaction designers:

Interaction designers create low-fidelity prototypes using Figma, test their designs with users, and document iterations in the Design History service to improve usability.

SEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • apply iterative design principles and agile methodologies to your work
  • iterate and improve a design based on successive rounds of research
  • prototype your ideas at different fidelities to explore and test designs
  • use a range of design tools and techniques
  • use and iterate design patterns and components

Service designers:

Service designers lead iterative improvements to service patterns, work with teams to refine service concepts based on user research, and establish frameworks for measuring the impact of iterations.

Interaction designers:

Interaction designers create prototypes using the GOV.UK prototype kit, learn and apply HTML, CSS and JavaScript where needed, conduct usability testing sessions, and maintain comprehensive documentation of design decisions in the Design History service.

G7

Practitioner

Capabilities:

  • help other designers apply iterative design principles and agile methodologies to their work
  • iterate and improve complex designs based on successive rounds of research
  • independently prototype complex ideas at an appropriate fidelity
  • adapt designs quickly to changes in requirements, priorities or user needs

Service designers:

Service designers set up prototype environments for multiple services, manage complex service iterations, and teach teams how to use prototyping tools effectively.

Interaction designers:

Interaction designers create complex prototypes using HTML, CSS and JavaScript in the GOV.UK prototype kit, mentor junior designers in frontend development, and lead iterative improvements based on user research and testing.

G6 Lead

Expert

Capabilities:

  • embed the practice of iterative design and agile working in teams or wider organisation
  • lead on prototyping complex ideas at any fidelity
  • help teams adapt quickly to changes in requirements, priorities or user needs
  • iterate delivery processes or team structures to help teams work effectively

Service designers:

Service designers work with interaction designers to define prototype requirements and scope, establish service patterns for testing, and ensure prototypes align with service strategy.

Interaction designers:

Interaction designers lead complex prototype development using HTML, CSS and JavaScript, establish coding standards for the GOV.UK prototype kit, and mentor teams in frontend development best practices.

G6 Head of Profession

Expert

Capabilities:

  • embed the practice of iterative design and agile working in teams or wider organisation
  • lead on prototyping complex ideas at any fidelity
  • help teams adapt quickly to changes in requirements, priorities or user needs
  • iterate delivery processes or team structures to help teams work effectively

Service designers:

Service designers work with interaction designers to define prototype requirements and scope, establish service patterns for testing, and ensure prototypes align with service strategy.

Interaction designers:

Interaction designers lead complex prototype development using HTML, CSS and JavaScript, establish coding standards for the GOV.UK prototype kit, and mentor teams in frontend development best practices.

Leading design

Leadership and guidance involves providing effective leadership and management through team motivation, decision-making, risk management, mediation, and professional development.

HEO

Awareness

Capabilities:

  • explain the role of user-centred design in an organisation
  • describe typical leadership responsibilities in design
  • identify leaders in your organisation that designers need to work with

Service designers:

Junior designers understand the role of design leadership and engage with senior leaders to ensure user-centred design is integrated into the service strategy.

Interaction designers:

Junior designers understand leadership within design teams and work closely with senior leaders to ensure design principles are embedded in the product and service design processes.

SEO

Working

Capabilities:

  • lead and coordinate design work in your team, with support
  • communicate the value of user-centred design to your team
  • support other designers
  • work with digital and data leaders in your organisation, when needed

Service designers:

Midweight designers solve whole problems by gathering insights around business culture, community networks, digital and offline environments, data, policy and technology. They facilitate collaboration by giving voice to different disciplines and creating spaces for solving problems together.

Interaction designers:

Midweight designers develop pattern libraries based on user research insights, help teams implement evidence-based design practices, and create reusable interaction components that address common user needs.

G7

Working

Capabilities:

  • lead and coordinate design work in your team, with support
  • communicate the value of user-centred design to your team
  • support other designers
  • work with digital and data leaders in your organisation, when needed

Service designers:

Senior designers lead the design of multiple services, coordinate with cross-functional teams, and mentor junior designers in delivering user-centred service design.

Interaction designers:

Senior designers lead the design and implementation of complex interaction flows, collaborate with stakeholders, and guide other designers in applying best practices for user interface design.

G6 Lead

Practitioner

Capabilities:

  • lead and coordinate design work in a team
  • advocate for user-centred design with leaders outside of digital and data
  • create an inclusive, productive environment for designers to work in
  • mentor and coach other designers
  • iterate and improve design processes within your team

Service designers:

Lead designers contribute to the development of DfE's design system, guide strategic design decisions across multiple services, and foster cross-functional collaboration at the leadership level.

Interaction designers:

Lead designers lead the design of complex systems and interfaces, manage design processes, and collaborate with senior leaders to ensure interaction design aligns with broader strategic goals.

G6 Head of Profession

Expert

Capabilities:

  • set a vision for and lead design across multiple teams or a whole organisation
  • work effectively with senior leaders across your organisation or wider public sector
  • support a culture of learning and experimentation in your organisation
  • iterate and measurably improve design maturity within your organisation and its partners

The Head of Profession:

The Head of Profession sets the vision and strategic direction for design, leading design teams across DfE, fostering a culture of learning and innovation, and representing design at senior levels across government.
