What UCD is

Put simply, user-centred design (UCD) is an iterative process that focuses on users and their needs at every phase of a project. This approach involves a multidisciplinary team working to better understand users and involving them in the design and decision-making process.

The ultimate aim is to create effective, efficient and good services and outcomes for people who use our services.

This guidance provides an overview of what UCD is and how it is utilised within the Department for Education.

It's Not a Straight Line

When people think of design, they often imagine a linear process. For example, come up with an idea, create a prototype, test it, and then launch it.

The iterative process of UCD

In reality, UCD is more like a treasure hunt. You might start off thinking you know what the 'treasure' is, but as you start to talk to users, understand their needs and problems, you might find yourself going off in different directions.

You will likely try options and solutions, test them with users, and keep learning. This is what we call an iteration.
