What UCD is

UCD process

  • Understand the user
  • Iterate and improve

Techniques

Iterating is vital for continually refining a product or service to meet and exceed user expectations. The approach is cyclical and encompasses several key phases, each of which is crucial for different reasons:

Low-fidelity prototypes

Initial iterations often start with simple, paper-based wireframes to sketch out various designs. This is a quick method to visualise your ideas.

High-fidelity prototypes

As you learn more about users, higher-fidelity prototypes can be developed, for example, using Figma. These look more like the final product and are a good first approach for more in-depth usability testing.

HTML prototypes

For more complex products or services, HTML, or interactive prototypes that look and behave like a real product can provide deeper insights and help identify potential usability challenges.

A/B testing

This type of testing compares two versions of particular features or patterns within the prototype to determine which performs better in terms of user experience.

Usability testing

Usability testing enables you to identify pain points in a design, and where there are opportunities to further eliminate friction in the user experience.

Qualitative and quantitive data

Using quantitative metrics such as time taken on page or on steps of a process through to qualitative feedback, comments and observations can help identify process improvements.
