Profession time in DfE

Every Thursday in DfE we have dedicated profession time. In practice, this means that between 1-4pm each profession can use some – or all – of this time for professional development.

Within this time, communities of practice meet between 1-2pm.

Then 2-4pm can be used for:

  • cross-design group objectives, for example, style guide or patterns
  • content, service and interaction design specific meet-ups
  • crits, either in profession or profession families
  • personal learning and development time

Join a DfE community of practice

Communities of practice help to drive the learning and development of the professions and their practice through sharing ideas, skills and experience.

The design community meets every Thursday between 1-2pm. This time is used to share cross-community information and learnings, for example, checking in with the cross-design working groups progress, or inviting cross-gov people to talk.

Once a month we have themes, and crowd source topics, facilitators and speakers. People can volunteer to chair and run a session. We also join up with other professions, such as user research or product to run multidisciplinary meet-ups. We encourage designers to set up user-centred profession meet-ups with their portfolio or business area, too.

To join the design community send a request to #design in DfE Slack.
