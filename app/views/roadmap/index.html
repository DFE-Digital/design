Purpose

The DfE Design Roadmap sets out the strategic direction for design, ensuring that digital services are consistent, accessible, and user-centred.

It provides a clear framework for embedding design excellence across DfE, ensuring that services meet high standards, reduce friction for users, and are built efficiently using shared principles, patterns, and evidence-based decision-making.

Design consistency and standards

Design consistency ensures that services across DfE follow a unified set of design principles and patterns, enabling a joined-up experience for users.

{% markdown %} ### Develop design consistency - Develop shared libraries of patterns, components, and templates for quicker and consistent design iterations - Support teams in aligning products with the Digital Product Branding standard - Ensuring we have robust design assurance to maintain consistency - Contribute more to the GOV.UK Design System and GOV.UK Service Manual ### Establish regular review and design crit sessions - Implement structured design critique sessions to share work and good practices - Foster a culture of constructive feedback and iteration, ensuring high design quality across teams {% endmarkdown %}

Accessibility

Accessibility is a legal requirement and a core design principle that ensures DfE digital services are usable by all users, including those with disabilities.

{% markdown %} ### Ensure accessibility compliance - Ensure digital products meet accessibility standards - Embed accessibility as a core requirement in all service delivery - Continue to develop accessibility guidelines, good practice and training resources - Develop a programme of continuous improvement for accessibility ### Embed accessibility into DfE's culture - Ensure accessibility is part of all design and development discussions - Encourage co-design with users of assistive technologies to build services that work for all - Make accessibility a leadership priority, embedding it in digital strategy and governance frameworks {% endmarkdown %}

Service mapping and user experience

Service mapping helps DfE visualise and understand the full journey of users across digital and non-digital services, improving decision-making and service design.

{% markdown %} ### Create a centralised service mapping repository - Establish a structured service map repository covering all DfE digital services - Ensure service maps are accessible for planning, development, and improvement - Use service maps to identify opportunities for service consolidation and improvement ### Improve visibility of user journeys - Map cross-service user journeys to highlight friction points and inefficiencies - Integrate service mapping insights into digital transformation planning ### Enable collaboration across teams - Break down silos by ensuring cross-functional teams contribute to service mapping - Share mapping insights to drive joined-up service delivery - Create a service-led design culture that prioritises outcomes over outputs {% endmarkdown %}

Metrics and performance measures

Developing shared performance measures helps DfE track and improve digital service quality, ensuring they meet user needs.

{% markdown %} ### Define shared performance metrics - Establish common metrics to measure usability, accessibility, and service effectiveness - Align performance tracking with DfE and GOV.UK service standards - Ensure all digital services have measurable success criteria ### Standardise user satisfaction and experience measures - Develop a consistent approach to user satisfaction and experience measures - Ensure all services have a user feedback mechanism - Use feedback to drive service improvements ### Standardise reporting and transparency - With product to create a shared performance dashboard - Ensure everyone has access to service design insights {% endmarkdown %}

Design assessor capability

Developing the talent in design assessors to be able to effectively assess products and services against standards and growing complexity.

{% markdown %} ### Strengthen design assessor training and expertise - Provide structured training for design assessors to develop skills and knowledge - Ensure assessors are knowledgeable across DfE and GOV.UK service standards and design principles - Ensure assessment findings lead to actionable improvements - Use survey responses to identify areas for improvement ### Track and evaluate the impact of design assessments - Measure how design assessments improve service quality and compliance with standards - Monitor assessment outcomes to identify recurring issues across services - Use insights from assessments to refine and evolve design standards {% endmarkdown %}

Training, skills, and capability

Continuing to develop the design skills capability framework in DfE and ensuring all designers can use the framework for personal development and have access to training.

{% markdown %} ### Develop structured training and career progression pathways - Create a clear learning pathway for designers at all levels, from early talent to senior roles - Provide training on design principles, accessibility, prototyping, and user research - Align training with DfE and GOV.UK standards to ensure consistency across services - Establish a mentorship programme to connect junior and senior designers ### Embed the design skills capability framework - Use the design skills capability framework to assess and support designers at all levels - Ensure all designers have role-specific objectives that align with the capability framework - Use data from the capability assessments to identify skill gaps and prioritise training {% endmarkdown %}
