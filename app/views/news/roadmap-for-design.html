{% markdown %} DesignOps have launched a [roadmap for design](/roadmap) to improve the consistency, accessibility, and effectiveness of digital services across the department. This initiative aims to create a more seamless experience for users, ensuring that digital services are well-designed, efficient, and meet the needs of everyone, including those with disabilities. The Design Roadmap will provide a structured, strategic approach to: * ensure design consistency by embedding shared principles, patterns, and a unified design system * improve accessibility compliance so that all services meet WCAG 2.2 AA standards * develop design capability and career progression pathways for designers across DfE * enhance user experience research and service mapping to create seamless, user-friendly digital services * introduce shared performance metrics to track design quality and usability across DfE ## Building a stronger design profession With this roadmap, we are making a commitment to delivering high-quality digital services that are easy to use, accessible to all, and designed with users in mind. The roadmap aligns with key DfE and GOV.UK objectives, ensuring that services are: * user-centred – Designed around real needs, based on research and data * efficient and scalable – Reducing duplication and improving cross-service consistency * compliant and inclusive – Meeting legal accessibility requirements and usability standards * innovative and future-proof – Supporting digital transformation across government ## What’s next? Over the coming months, we will: * expand the DfE Design System, ensuring common components and styles are easily reusable * strengthen accessibility training and support for all digital teams * improve service mapping and user journey mapping * develop objectives and personal development plans for all designers Learn more about the [roadmap](/roadmap). {% endmarkdown %}
