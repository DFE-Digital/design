Content design {% markdown %} # {{ title }} {{ description }} ## GOV.UK mainstream content This is content for the public and is managed by GDS (Government Digital Service). We work with GDS to produce content for parents and carers, covering services such as looking for childcare or a school place. Examples of mainstream content: * [types of school](https://www.gov.uk/types-of-school) * [school admissions](https://www.gov.uk/schools-admissions) * [school term and holiday dates](https://www.gov.uk/school-term-holiday-dates) ## GOV.UK specialist content This content is managed by our digital communications team (DigiComms). You can find out more about [working with DigiComms on the DfE intranet](https://educationgovuk.sharepoint.com/sites/communications/SitePages/publish-gov-uk.aspx). Specialist content is used to publish statutory guidance, policy papers, and detailed guides for professionals. We also publish some guidance for parents and press releases. Example of specialist content: * [plan your relationships, sex and health curriculum](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/plan-your-relationships-sex-and-health-curriculum) * [schools: statutory guidance](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/statutory-guidance-schools) * [keeping children safe in education](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/keeping-children-safe-in-education--2) ## Services hosted on the service.gov.uk domain These services are for the public and must meet the [GOV.UK proposition](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/govuk-proposition/govuk-proposition) to be provided on the service.gov.uk domain. Example of services on service.gov.uk: * [Apply for teacher training](https://www.gov.uk/apply-for-teacher-training) * [Get information about schools](https://get-information-schools.service.gov.uk/) * [Compare school performance](https://www.compare-school-performance.service.gov.uk/) ## Services hosted on the education domain These services are for professionals and provided on the education.gov.uk domain. They may then move to GOV.UK once they are live and nationally available. Example of a service on education.gov.uk: * [Help for early years providers](https://help-for-early-years-providers.education.gov.uk/) ## Campaign sites These sites are managed by the [Government Communications Service (GCS)](https://gcs.civilservice.gov.uk/) (some are managed by third-party providers). They are aimed at the public and are used for a specific time period and have a specific objective. Example of a campaign site: * [Get into teaching](https://getintoteaching.education.gov.uk/) * [Early years careers campaign](https://earlyyearscareers.campaign.gov.uk/) [Contact DigiComms (DfE intranet)](https://educationgovuk.sharepoint.com/sites/communications/SitePages/publish-gov-uk.aspx) if you think you need a campaign site. ## Communication channels We also use several communication channels to engage with different audiences and direct users to our services and guidance. These include: * [GOV.UK Notify](https://www.notifications.service.gov.uk/) for emails and text messages sent from digital products * [blogs](https://dfedigital.blog.gov.uk/) * sector newsletters for professionals and stakeholders * social media {% endmarkdown %}
