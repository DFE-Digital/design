Content design {% markdown %} # {{title}} We conduct a variety of reviews and checks ahead of publishing content. As you draft content in various formats, such as Word, Figma, Lucid, or Whitehall content publisher, you'll need to complete regular reviews on your content and designs. ## Reviews and content crits You can organise a review or content critique (crit) yourself through the various DfE Slack channels for design, and ask people to attend using a Teams call. You can also join one of the [cross-government design Slack channels](https://ukgovernmentdigital.slack.com/archives/C062GAGLW). To run review or crit: * provide the context of the content * explain the feedback you're looking for * explain how you want the feedback, for example, in comments or edits on your document It’s useful to timebox activities (set a timer for each activity) and, if on a large call, consider using breakout rooms to encourage discussion. It can be helpful to do review or crits in person using post-its and printouts. There is helpful advice in the [blog post on using design crits to improve collaboration](https://designnotes.blog.gov.uk/2017/11/27/using-design-crits-to-improve-collaboration/). ## Prior to the 2i check A 2i check (meaning a second pair of eyes) should be done on the final version in context, usually in the production or test environment, or in a preview on a content management system. Before the 2i, check you’ve done the following: * named the service or publication following [GOV.UK naming conventions](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/design/naming-your-service) * checked the factual accuracy with a policy or subject-matter expert * have [clearance to publish (DfE intranet)](https://educationgovuk.sharepoint.com/sites/communications/SitePages/publish-gov-uk.aspx) – this will vary by project * have a publication or ‘go live’ plan If the content needs to change while preparing the final version in the content management system, you must check that it's still accurate. The 2i check is the final check. ## 2i checks by content type You may need to vary the checks based on the type of content and where it's published. For example: * name of content, service or page * start page * branded headers and footers * statement pages, such as privacy and accessibility * components * style There is guidance on [how to make your service look like GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/design/making-your-service-look-like-govuk). There is also guidance in this manual for how to apply the DfE branded [header](/design-system/components/header). ### Checks for GOV.UK specific publication For example: * title * template * collection and taxonomy tagging * change notes There is GOV.UK guidance on how to [review and publish content in Whitehall publisher](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/how-to-publish-on-gov-uk/reviewing-and-approving-content). This also includes guidance on what we, as a government department, should and should not publish. ### Checks for manuals and guidance (including technical guides) For example: * name * titles * signposting from specialist content and collections There is a section for manuals in the [GOV.UK planning, writing and managing content guide](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/content-design/content-types). {% endmarkdown %}
