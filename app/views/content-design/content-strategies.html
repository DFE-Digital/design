Content design {% markdown %} # {{ title }} {{ description }} A content strategy helps you create and maintain content that meets user needs and organisational goals. It ensures efficient content planning, collaboration, and the measurement of content success. The strategy should give an overview of the products or services covered, the users, and purpose. ## Before you start Read the following guidance before you start your strategy: - [DfE GOV.UK content strategy (DfE intranet)](https://educationgovuk.sharepoint.com/sites/communications/SitePages/dfe-govuk-content-strategy.aspx?) - [DfE strategy and vision (DfE intranet)](https://educationgovuk.sharepoint.com/sites/strategy-and-vision) Work with your team to identify: - the goals for your users - how content will help them meet those goals - how you'll measure success of the product Record how you'll meet these goals while creating, testing, and iterating content. Consider including a [stakeholder map](https://analysisfunction.civilservice.gov.uk/policy-store/stakeholder-mapping/) to track key contributors, such as subject-matter experts, policy experts, developers, and business analysts. ## Monitor and measure Monitor the success of your strategy in the following ways: - user testing - insights from usage data about journeys across content or through your service - feedback loops Work with the team to define benchmarks and how you'll measure them. Examples include: - % of users landing on a page from a search result - number of issues reported with accuracy of information - % of errors in links - number of times content is updated over a period of time Your should agree with the team how you will publish [service performance data](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/measuring-success) alongside your key performance indicators (KPIs). ## Plan how you'll quality assure your content This should include: - how often you'll identify, develop, and test content before publishing - how to get sign-off for content - how you'll ensure content is accurate and up-to-date ## Agree governance and ownership The content strategy should be written and owned by the content designer working on the product or service. It should be agreed by all relevant stakeholders. This should include: - ensuring factual accuracy of the content - how changes are agreed - content maintenance ## Review and iterate the strategy As you learn more about your users you should iterate your content strategy. This is especially important when moving to a new project phase, such as from discovery to alpha, or if the product or service vision changes. Over time, you'll gain a deeper understanding of your service and users. Your data and reporting will help you refine how you measure success, which should be reflected in the strategy. You should also agree on a strategy for archiving or removing content that is no longer needed. ## Further reading [Everything you need to do a content strategy - Content Design London](https://www.contentdesign.london/blog/everything-you-need-to-do-content-strategy) [The importance of end-to-end strategy - Content Design London](https://contentdesign.london/blog/the-importance-of-end-to-end-strategy) {% endmarkdown %}
