The Inside design projects area of the DfE design manual allows digital teams to publish project updates for external stakeholders, and people who use our services.

Purpose

The content you add to your Inside Design page should be specific to your product or service. It should give an update on the status of your work.

The purpose of the page is to keep people informed and offer opportunities for people to get involved through show and tells, research, or webinars.

By working in the open, you can involve people who may not be in your user group that are affected by your services.

! Warning You should only include information that can be shared publicly and should not include sensitive information.

Providing project updates on your work can help make sure our products and services are fit for purpose. They help us to deliver to the DfE strategy and vision and meet peopleâ€™s needs.

Planning

You need to have a detailed plan for the launch and maintenance of your Inside Design content.

The plan should:

  • Mirror your delivery plan
  • Cover key stages and deliverables
  • Consider related policy and departmental activity

Your page should be updated to give details on the project now. It is not a mechanism to document the history of the project, you can do this through things like design histories. It is a medium to use to inform and engage others in current project activities.

Risks

There is always a risk when publishing information. You should consider how you mitigate those risks, through your publishing and sign off process, planning, and signposting people who may wish to raise a concern.

Approval

As with everything you publish publicly, you need to have sign off at Deputy Director (DD) level. DDs can delegate approval, this should be reviewed every 12 months. You should also consider whether to liaise with central DfE communication colleagues, depending on the nature and sensitivity of the project.

Launch

You should already have an established list of stakeholders and interested parties. You should know who you are aiming to inform and engage and understand what they need from your updates.

You may want to consider holding a webinar as part of your launch activities, along with using emails or the sector newsletters. It may even be appropriate to consider a post on the DfE digital blog.

You can add images and recordings of things like webinars to your page.

Wider strategy

Our digital delivery projects sit under strategic aims and policy areas. We should direct to these where appropriate and be sure to monitor updates and consider any impact on our content. This could include updating your content strategy.
