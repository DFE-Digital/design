Content design {% markdown %} # {{title}} The name of your product or service is important. A good name makes it easier for people to: * find your service * understand what it does * know what outcome they can achieve by using it Policy and service teams should follow guidance in the GOV.UK Service Manual for [naming your service](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/design/naming-your-service). This guidance applies to both internal and external services. ## Do not use terms like portal, hub, or other technical jargon
! Warning Your service could be prevented from going into beta or live and even be required to be taken offline if it does not have an appropriate name.
You should avoid calling your service things like 'portal', 'hub', 'dashboard', or similar abstract terms. These words: * are not plain language, most users do not use or understand them * do not describe what the service does, users should know what they can do just by reading the name * add cognitive load, especially for users with lower digital confidence or neurodivergence * often feel vague or corporate * risk excluding users or causing confusion ## You'll be asked to demonstrate how you named your service Your service will go through either a discovery peer review or a service assessment. By the end of discovery, you should have proposed a working name. During alpha, you must test the name with users, and be confident that: * it accurately reflects what the service does * users understand it without explanation * it aligns with [GOV.UK naming conventions](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/choose-your-govuk-domain-name) ## Standards you must meet Your service name will be reviewed as part of meeting the following standards: * [Digital Product Branding (DDTS-301)](https://standards.education.gov.uk/standard/digital-product-branding) * [Plain Language standard (DDTS-529)](https://standards.education.gov.uk/standard/plain-language) * [GOV.UK Service Standard](https://www.gov.uk/service-manual/service-standard) This means your service name must: * use simple, clear words * be understandable to all users * avoid jargon, acronyms, and internal terms ## Resources to help you name your service Posts and examples to provide context and evidence for naming services: * [How the 'Teacher self-service portal' was renamed](https://design-histories.education.gov.uk/access-your-teaching-qualifications/naming-the-service), can help run a service naming workshop * [Naming the Manage an academy transfer service](https://dfedigital.blog.gov.uk/2022/03/11/how-to-name-a-service/), is an example of naming an internal service * [Good services are verbs, bad services are nouns](https://designnotes.blog.gov.uk/2015/06/22/good-services-are-verbs-2/), gov guidance to name your service {% endmarkdown %}
