The standard

When writing content for any digital media, content must meet the four principles of the plain language standard.

The four principles are:

  • readers can get what they need (relevant)
  • readers can find what they need (findable)
  • readers can understand what they find (understandable)
  • readers can use the information (usable)

You can work to meet this standard by following the GOV.UK style guide and DfE style guide and guidance within the content design section of this manual.

How this standard is assured

In addition to reporting compliance through the standards dashboard, this standard will also be assured through the service assessment process at Beta phase, through Service Standard Point 5 - Make sure everyone can use the service.

You should provide a copy of your compliance report to the design assessor, you can do this by adding a link to it in the artefacts section of the Service assessment service when you are being assessed.

Declare your conformance

You can reach different levels of conformance with this standard.

Not met
You can evidence meeting less than half the guidelines
Partially met
You can evidence meeting more than half, but not all, guidelines
Fully met
You can evidence meeting all the guidelines

Evidencing conformance

When you declare your conformance to this standard on the service reporting dashboard, you must provide evidence of compliance using the conformance report template for this standard.

Once you have completed your conformance report for a product or service, you must email a copy of your report to the DesignOps inbox in addition to reporting it on the standards dashboard.

You must do this when your product enters private beta, public beta or beta (whichever comes first).

Your conformance report must also show any actions you are taking to meet any instances of non-compliance, and be reviewed on a regular basis, and at most, every 12 months.
