Structure the document for readers

We work with user researchers to find out what's important to the users. We then design content that helps users find the information they need.

We structure content so it answers what most people want to know first.

You can meet this by working to the following Service Standard points:

  • 1. Understand users and their needs
  • 4. Make the service simple to use

Additional guidance, patterns or components to support meeting this standard:

  • Warning text - GOV.UK Design System

Use information design techniques that enable readers to find information

We design content using approved GOV.UK components and patterns, which help users see what the page is about, navigate the page and make decisions about what to do next.

We use DfE specific patterns and components, where appropriate.

You can meet this by working to the following Service Standard points:

  • 1. Understand users and their needs
  • 4. Make the service simple to use
  • 5. Make sure everyone can use the service
  • 13. Use and contribute to open patterns, common components and patterns

Additional guidance, patterns or components to support meeting this standard:

  • GOV.UK style guide
  • DfE style guide
  • GOV.UK Design system
  • DfE Design system

Use headings to help readers predict what comes next

We use headings and sub-headings to help users see what the page is about and to navigate the content.

We optimise content for the search engines by including relevant search terms in the headings.

You can meet this by working to the following Service Standard points:

  • 1. Understand users and their needs
  • 4. Make the service simple to use

Additional guidance, patterns or components to support meeting this standard:

  • Writing for GOV.UK
  • Headings (GOV.UK Design system)

Keep supplementary information separate

We give people the information they need at the most appropriate time in their journey.

We link users to further information, rather than duplicating information or providing everything upfront.

You can meet this by working to the following Service Standard points:

  • 4. Make the service simple to use
